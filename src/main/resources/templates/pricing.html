Questions

What's the difference between a Trip Pass and Nomad?

A Trip Pass is a one-time $3 payment that unlocks everything for 14 days. It's perfect for a single journey, with no recurring charge. Nomad is a monthly subscription for people who travel often.

Where does the data come from?

Traveler reports, local news and official advisories, structured with AI and linked back to their sources. RoamSafe summarizes what's real. It never invents a statistic.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. Nomad can be cancelled whenever you like, and you keep access until the end of the period. A Trip Pass simply expires after 14 days, so there's nothing to cancel.

Is neighborhood-level data available everywhere?

Coverage is expanding. Well-reported cities have district-level detail today. Others start at city level and deepen as travelers contribute. We label what we have, and never overstate it.
